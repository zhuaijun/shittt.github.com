This year the qualification round consisted of 4 problems. As lately I have been using Ruby I decided to try my luck with it, and avoid writing all the Java boilerplate code to do simple things like reading and parsing a text file. I am using Ubuntu 12.04 installed in VirtualBox 4.1.12

The competition started at 01:00 AM in my country, but nevertheless I stayed up and tried (and solved) at least one of the problems before sleep kicked in. These are the solutions I came up with under the circumstances.

Problem A: Speaking in Tongues

This one was the first I tried, and in my opinion the easiest of the set. The explanation and the hints made very clear what was to do. This solution gave me 15 points:

Problem B: Dancing With the Googlers

I think I overcomplicated this one, but I managed to obtain a solution, not a final one as I missed a case, because the large input failed.

My solution solved the small input, but failed with the large one, so this gave me only 10 points.

Problem C: Recycled Numbers

This one was a bit tricky because there were some unspecified cases that the solution had to deal with, like the permutations of same digit numbers (1111 is the same as 1111 although some of the 1's are exchanged) which you were not supposed to consider.

This solved the small input, and eventually the large one, but not in the required 8 minutes (it took ~11 minutes). So I only gained 10 points for the small input.

I had no time left to try the last one, but with the accumulated score of 35 I qualified for the next round, so I am happy with that.

I think that for the next round I will install ruby natively on my windows machine, to gain some CPU power for the large inputs, and maybe be able to solve them in time.

See you on April 28th on Round 1!
